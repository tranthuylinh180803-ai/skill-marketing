# Threads Draft — Mental Health & Identity
**Topic:** Gen Z / Millennial mental health culture in America  
**Tone:** Relatable / funny / self-narrative prose  
**Platform:** Threads (Meta)  
**Format:** 10-post thread, each post = 1 flowing paragraph, ≤ 500 chars

---

## POST 1 — Hook

We are the first generation to have unlimited access to mental health information, and somehow we are more anxious than ever. I think about this a lot. 🧵

---

## POST 2 — Setup the irony

I know what a trauma response looks like. I know the difference between anxiety and an overactive nervous system. I've read the threads, watched the videos, done the workbook. And I still panic over an unanswered text at 9pm like it's a matter of survival.

---

## POST 3 — Therapy speak observation

The other day my roommate told me she couldn't come to dinner because she "needed to protect her energy." My roommate. Not a therapist. Not a wellness influencer. Just a 24-year-old who used to cancel plans by saying she was tired. Something has shifted.

---

## POST 4 — TikTok irony

I spent 40 minutes on TikTok last Tuesday watching videos about how doomscrolling is ruining our generation's mental health. Then I kept scrolling. I'm not proud of it. But I also think at least 60% of you have done the exact same thing, so.

---

## POST 5 — Relatable specific moment

Before my first therapy appointment, I had already diagnosed myself with ADHD, rejection sensitive dysphoria, and unresolved childhood emotional neglect. My therapist listened, nodded, and told me I was just underpaid and sleeping 5 hours a night. I wanted to argue. I couldn't.

---

## POST 6 — Defend the generation

To be fair, no generation before us had to compare themselves to literally every human on the internet in real time, while also being expected to perform happiness about it. We didn't invent anxiety. We just got handed more of it with fewer tools and a comment section.

---

## POST 7 — Healing era aesthetic

At some point "healing" stopped being something you do and became something you are. You don't go to therapy, you're "in your healing era." You don't take a nap, you're "honoring your body's need for rest." I'm not saying it's wrong. I'm saying I've used both of those sentences unironically this week.

---

## POST 8 — The real insight

The thing that worries me a little is that there's a difference between naming your emotions and actually sitting with them. We've gotten very good at saying "I'm in a triggered state right now" and then opening Instagram. That's not healing. That's just a more articulate form of avoidance.

---

## POST 9 — Solidarity

None of this means we're failing. It means we're the first people trying to understand human psychology at the exact same time we're living it, in public, with notifications on. That's a genuinely hard thing to do, and most of us are doing it without a manual.

---

## POST 10 — CTA

If any of this felt a little too specific — follow. I write about the weird, honest parts of being alive right now. No advice columns. No 5-step fixes. Just the observations.
