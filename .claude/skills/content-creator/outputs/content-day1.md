Raise your hand if your bathroom counter starts perfectly organized on Sunday and looks like a tornado hit it by Thursday 🙋

I used to think I just wasn't an "organized person." Like it was some personality trait I was born without and other people got it instead.

But here's what I figured out — it's not about being organized. It's about having a setup that makes putting things back easier than leaving them out.

Three things that actually helped me:

→ Keep everything visible. If I have to open a drawer to find it, I won't put it back in the drawer. Out of sight = out of routine.

→ Group by your routine, not by category. Face stuff together. Hair stuff together. Not "all liquids" or "all bottles."

→ Counter space is earned. If something lives on the counter, it has to pull its weight every single day. Everything else goes under the sink.

Still a work in progress over here, not gonna lie. But mornings are genuinely less chaotic now and that alone is worth it.

5 things probably living on your bathroom counter that have zero business being there 😅

1. Products you've used twice and feel too guilty to throw away
2. Something your partner put there "temporarily" six months ago
3. A candle that's never been lit
4. Three versions of the same product because you couldn't decide
5. The cap to something you lost in December

No judgment — this was literally my counter last month.

The rule that changed everything: if I haven't touched it in 30 days, it doesn't get counter space. Under the sink, in a drawer, or trash. Pick one.

Your counter real estate is prime. Treat it like it is.

Not gonna lie, I used to scroll past those perfectly organized bathroom counter photos and think "must be nice."

Clean surfaces. Everything matching. Looked like nobody actually lived there.

Meanwhile mine had: dry shampoo from two seasons ago, approximately 40 hair ties, a toothbrush holder that had seen better days, and mystery residue I refuse to think about too hard.

But here's what I finally figured out — those organized bathrooms aren't the way they are because those people are fundamentally different from me. They just removed the daily decision-making.

When everything has a spot that actually makes sense, putting it back requires zero thought. When it doesn't have a spot? You set it down "just for now." And now it's been three months.

Here's the part nobody really talks about though: it's not just about the counter looking nice. It's about how you feel at 7am when you're already half-asleep trying to get yourself and possibly your kids out the door.

A calm counter is a calmer start. And some mornings, that's everything.

Okay I'm clearly having a lot of feelings about bathroom organization today 😂 but if you're nodding along, you are definitely not alone.
